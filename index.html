About

Geospatial analyst bridging remote sensing, GIS, and ecological insight to solve complex environmental challenges.

I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in Forestry - Remote Sensing and GIS in the Department of Forestry at Mississippi State University . My work focuses on forest biometrics, geospatial data science, and ecological research, applying quantitative tools to address interdisciplinary environmental questions.

As a Graduate Research Assistant in the Ecophysiology Lab, I contribute to independent and collaborative projects that integrate remote sensing plant physiology, GIS, and geo-spatial analysis. My training combines machine learning and statistical modeling with hands-on data processing in R and Python.

I have experience in geospatial data science, geodatabase management, and cartographic visualization, including processing UAV‑hyperspectral imagery and performing spatial analysis with ArcGIS Pro, ArcMap, ENVI, and Python. I have managed projects in disaster mapping and hydrological modeling, and I have delivered GIS training workshops.

My research interests center on using geospatial data to tackle environmental and ecological challenges. I aim to leverage advanced GIS, remote sensing, and modeling skills to support evidence‑based decision‑making in environmental management and planning.

Beyond research, I enjoy playing volleyball, watching anime and movies, traveling, and playing the musical instrument bansuri.
